Companies platform

Creating clear information architecture for easier user navigation in the companies platform

Introduction

Recur Club is a financial platform that provides hassle-free financing for startups. It offers a variety of benefits, including:

Quick and easy access to capital: Recur Club can provide startups with the capital they need to grow and succeed, quickly and easily.
Flexible repayment options: Recur Club offers flexible repayment options that are tailored to the needs of each startup.
No collateral required: Recur Club does not require any collateral, making it a good option for startups with limited assets.
Expert support: Recur Club provides expert support to startups throughout the financing process.

Back Story

The company product is one of the products among the investors portal, admin, and insights products which was taking shape after 1 year of operations. Initially, the customer success team and business developments used to handle the startup that came for debt financing. But as the company was approached by a huge number of startups than companies in onboarding was getting very difficult to handle so a project was taken up to build a seamless product experience for the two personas one was the founder and the other was the finance manager's persona.

Problem Identification

At first, I understood the initial workflow of all the modules and built the entire information architecture so that I could use that to frame my user interview questions. I have gone through the Hotjar recordings on each module and listed

All the findings and the pain points of the user.
Dropoff of users on each page
Average time spent by a user on each page.
Understood the user paths to complete one task.
Through heatmap understand the user behavior with the product.

I have used the Mixpanel tool to understand the user behavior like after signup when the user came back. Conducted the dropoff analysis in each workflow.

Using Qualitative Methods

There was a process set by the organization to talk to companies regularly and take timely feedback on the product's journey
After going through the hot jar recording and the mix panel user flow analysis I have made a couple of questions as a list of questions to be asked along with understanding the user pain points.


Example of list of questions

Understanding the signup process.
Where do get all the required files to complete the onboarding?
How do they add the finance manager to complete the onboarding process?
What time does it take to get all those files?
What are the tasks that people are doing after post-listing?
How they are making Repayments?
Why do they use payments and payouts? and how frequently
What do people do in the collections module?
How do they make financial reports using our product?
How they are tracking their repayments? etc
Conducted User interviews and found all the pain points and understood their expectations.

Conducted the UX review

After understanding the current user behavior and all the pain points. i have done a UX review on all the pages by using Jakob Nielsen's heuristics


Restructured the information architecture




After all the findings, build the visual design for the new navigational flow

After restructuring the product, the number of queries was reduced, and we received positive feedback from customers. We started working on new feature requests and process standardization.
